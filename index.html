Why Write?

Fictional writing is a rewarding hobby. Not only do authors get to express themselves through their stories, they get to expand their scope of creativity by exploring different characters, settings, themes, ideas, developments, and so much more. An added benefit to writing is the extra practice of the English language, which leads to a broader vocabulary and more ease in writing other projects such as essays or reports. For authors who do historical fiction, the added research put in to the period in which the story is can be helpful for learning more history. Writing is not just about telling a story. It is about creating a world, communicating ideas, and adding to personal learning in a fun and innovative way.

About the Author

Writing fictional stories has always been a passion of mine since I was only seven. My older brother first wrote a short story about a character in a video game, and it started all four siblings writing. While most of my siblings left writing, I continued on, constantly looking for inspiration and ideas for new stories. In the early years of my writing, I never dreamed of how much it would help me later on. When I took my first semester in college, I was only sixteen and the youngest in my class. One of the first classes I took was English Composition, and I was terrified as I had never written a college essay before. However, I soon found that essays were easy for me, as I was able to express myself and my ideas clearly through writing, and I ended up enjoying that class immensely. To this day, I still have many ideas and characters that I would love to share through fictional writing.
